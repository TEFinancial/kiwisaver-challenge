KiwiSaver Challenge

Can you beat the professionals?

Every year thousands of New Zealanders change KiwiSaver funds, often based on recent performance.

This challenge explores how difficult it really is to consistently choose the best-performing fund using only the information that would have been available at the time.

1

Begin with $100,000

No additional contributions are made during the challenge. Your goal is to grow your investment over time.

2

Choose from anonymous funds

Each year choose one of ten real KiwiSaver growth funds hidden behind Greek letters: Alpha, Beta, Gamma, and so on.

3

Decide with available information

After each year you see your return, updated portfolio value, and the returns of all ten anonymous funds.

4

Share why you chose

Each decision asks why you made the choice. There are no right or wrong answers.

5

Receive a professional report

At the end you receive a personalised investment behaviour report comparing your strategy to real fund outcomes.

ℹ️ Disclaimer

This challenge has been created for educational purposes only to demonstrate how investment decisions and investor behaviour can influence long-term outcomes. It is not intended to provide financial advice or recommend any particular investment provider or investment strategy.

The historical returns used in this challenge have been compiled from Morningstar, publicly available Product Disclosure Statements (PDSs) and fund information published by the relevant fund managers. Returns shown are before tax and fees.

Where a fund did not exist for a particular historical year, the market index return has been used for that year to provide a consistent comparison across all funds.

Historical performance figures may differ slightly from those published elsewhere due to differences in reporting periods, calculation methodologies, rounding and data sources. The figures used in this challenge should be regarded as reasonable approximations for educational purposes.

Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future performance, and future investment returns are not guaranteed.
